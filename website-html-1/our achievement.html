Achievement for our Organization

It's important to develop achievement levels in your organization to give the employees goals to which they can ascribe. A goal is a benchmark in the future and provides a level of motivation and recognition for exceptional work. Achievement levels provide measurable landmarks of your progress through the organization

Many companies use titles to designate achievement levels in their organizations. Often with the titles comes additional responsibility. At other times, the title signifies a raise in pay or recognition of time spent in the company. Senior sales associate and assistant manager often are titles that carry little extra duties but recognize an employee's level of service. Senior vice president, partner and managing director are titles to which employees can aspire that usually come with new job descriptions and substantially more responsibilities and pay.
